Early Life

Linus Torvalds was born in Helsinki, Finland, in 1969. He developed an early interest in computers and programming, inspired by his family. He later studied computer science at the University of Helsinki.

Creation of Linux

In 1991, Linus created the Linux kernel as a personal project. It quickly grew into one of the most important open-source operating systems in the world, powering servers, smartphones, and supercomputers.

Creation of Git

In 2005, Linus developed Git, a distributed version control system. Today, Git is the backbone of modern software development and is used by millions of developers worldwide.

Personality & Leadership

Linus is known for his direct communication style and strong opinions. Despite controversy at times, he remains a respected leader in the open-source community.

Legacy & Impact

Linus Torvalds changed the world of computing through Linux and Git. His work enabled the growth of open-source software and modern internet infrastructure.
